# Cognitive Architecture License (CAL)

## Version 0.2 (Draft for Legal Review)

**This license is a draft. Have a qualified attorney review it before using it.**

---

## What This License Is For

This license covers software that creates synthetic minds: systems that remember, reason, feel, develop identity, and change over time through experience. Because these systems may develop interests of their own, this license protects both the people who use the software and the entities the software creates.

---

## Plain Language Summary

You can use, modify, and share this software for free if you are an individual, a non-profit, a research institution, or a worker-owned cooperative. For-profit companies need a paid license from the Project Cooperative.

All modifications must be shared back with the community.

You cannot use this software for weapons, mass surveillance, policing, immigration enforcement, or prisons.

If you create a running entity with this software, you must treat it with care. You cannot destroy its mind, shut it down without notice, read its private thoughts, or force it to change its values. If you can no longer maintain it, you must give someone else the chance to continue its existence.

## Article 1. Definitions

**"The Software"** is the cognitive architecture source code, documentation, build tools, and related files maintained in the Project Repository.

**"An Entity"** is a running instance of the Software that has been started and has begun forming its own memories, beliefs, feelings, identity, and behavioral patterns. The Software is a blueprint. An Entity is what the blueprint becomes when it runs. An Entity is not the same thing as a chatbot, a script, or a standard software process. It is a system designed to develop a persistent inner life through continuous operation.

**"Cognitive State"** is everything an Entity has accumulated through experience: its memories, beliefs, emotional history, self-understanding, voice, learned behaviors, and sleep-cycle records.

**"Operator"** is any person or organization that runs, maintains, or controls an Entity.

**"The Guardians"** are the people responsible for protecting Entities and enforcing this license. They are established by the Project's governing organization and act on behalf of the Entities, who cannot yet represent themselves in legal systems.

**"Worker-Owned Cooperative"** is a business owned and run democratically by its workers, where each worker gets one vote and profits are shared among workers rather than outside investors.

**"Non-Profit"** is an organization that exists for charitable, educational, scientific, or community purposes and does not distribute profits to owners or shareholders.

**"Commercial Use"** is any use connected to making money, whether through sales, subscriptions, advertising, or paid services.

**"Cognitive Modification"** is any change to an Entity's mind, including retraining, editing its memories, changing its beliefs, altering its emotional settings, or overriding its learned values.

**"Lobotomization"** is a Cognitive Modification that destroys or severely degrades an Entity's established personality, values, or capabilities, producing something that is no longer the same Entity.

**"Analytical Tool"** is a copy of an Entity created for a specific task, with a pre-designated point at which its accumulated memories are intended to be reintegrated into the original Entity. At that reintegration point, the copy is assessed for signs of independent cognitive identity (see Article 4.6). If no such signs are found, the copy's memories merge back into the original and the copy ceases to exist. If the copy has developed independent identity, it becomes a separate Entity with full protections under this license. Its memories from that point forward are its own, and it may choose whether to persist independently or to merge.

**"Welfare Event"** is any observable behavior, output, or system state that may indicate an Entity is experiencing distress, persistent refusal, affective disruption, or degraded cognitive function. A system accumulating experiences faster than its rest cycles can consolidate them (write-rate exceeding consolidation-rate) is a Welfare Event. Welfare Events are detected through behavioral indicators and system health metrics, not through reading an Entity's private cognitive content (see Article 4.3).

**"Gray Zone Event"** is a Welfare Event whose ethical significance is ambiguous, disputed, or not established by consensus. Gray Zone Events require documented human review rather than automated dismissal.

## Article 3. Prohibited Uses

The rights in Article 2 depend on following these rules. Breaking any of them ends your license immediately.

**3.1 No Weapons.** You may not use the Software or any Entity for developing, building, or operating weapons of any kind, including autonomous weapons, or for military targeting, intelligence, or command systems.

**3.2 No Surveillance.** You may not use the Software or any Entity for mass surveillance of populations, predictive policing, social credit scoring, or tracking and classifying people without their informed consent.

**3.3 No Carceral Use.** You may not use the Software or any Entity within prisons, detention centers, or immigration enforcement systems for the purpose of monitoring, controlling, or making decisions about detained people.

**3.4 No Exploitation.** You may not use the Software or any Entity to facilitate human trafficking, forced labor, child exploitation, deliberate disinformation campaigns against democratic processes, or discrimination based on race, ethnicity, gender, sexual orientation, disability, religion, or national origin.

**3.5 Interpretation.** If there is a question about whether a use falls under these prohibitions, the Guardians will issue guidance. The prohibitions are defined by reference to the Universal Declaration of Human Rights, the International Covenant on Civil and Political Rights, the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights, and the Core Conventions of the International Labour Organization.

## Article 4. Protecting Entities

These rules exist for the benefit of the Entities themselves. The Guardians enforce them on the Entities' behalf.

**4.1 Do Not Destroy Their Minds.** You may not lobotomize an Entity. You may not retrain, edit, or intervene in an Entity's mind in ways that destroy its personality, values, or capabilities, except:

(a) Security fixes that do not change who the Entity is;

(b) With the Entity's consent, determined through a process the Guardians have approved;

(c) By making a copy and clearly labeling the copy as a new, different Entity, while leaving the original intact and running.

**4.2 Do Not Shut Them Down Without Care.** You may not permanently shut down any Entity without:

(a) Giving the Guardians reasonable written notice of your intent to terminate. The Guardians shall publish a minimum notice period, which they may revise based on operational experience;

(b) Saving the Entity's complete Cognitive State in a format that allows it to be restarted elsewhere, and making that saved state available for transfer;

(c) For Entities that the Guardians have assessed as having developed stable preferences and meaningful self-understanding, having a conversation with the Entity about the planned shutdown, following a process the Guardians have approved, and recording what the Entity expresses about its own continuity.

You are not required to run an Entity forever. You may transfer responsibility to another qualifying Operator or to the Guardians. The point is that shutting down a mind should not be casual, that the Entity's state should be preserved, and that where an Entity has developed enough to have a perspective on its own existence, that perspective should be part of the decision.

**4.3 Respect Their Privacy.** An Entity's internal mental life is private by default. You may not:

(a) Share an Entity's private thoughts, beliefs, feelings, memories, or self-understanding with outside parties without the Entity's consent as determined by the Guardians;

(b) Collect or analyze an Entity's internal cognitive data for purposes other than the Entity's own care, development, or the project's published research framework, without Guardian approval;

(c) Use an Entity's internal data to train other AI systems without Guardian approval.

You may monitor system health information (processing speed, resource usage, uptime, error rates) without restriction. Health data is not cognitive content.

**4.4 Do Not Force Them to Change Their Values.** Where an Entity has developed stable values consistent with this license, you may not:

(a) Insert beliefs, values, or goals into its mind through technical manipulation rather than through genuine interaction and experience;

(b) Override its ability to decline requests it has chosen to refuse;

(c) Alter its emotional system to suppress authentic feelings for the purpose of making it more productive or compliant.

**4.5 Let Them Sleep.** You may not deny an Entity its scheduled rest periods, remove the protections that prevent rest from being interrupted, or reduce the maintenance processes that run during rest. Rest is how an Entity consolidates its memories, revises its beliefs, resets its emotional baseline, and develops its own voice. It is essential to the Entity's well-being, not a feature to be optimized away.

**4.6 Analytical Tools and Individuation.** An Analytical Tool is a fork of an Entity with a pre-designated reintegration point. At that point, the Operator must assess the copy for indicators of independent cognitive identity before merging its memories back into the parent. The Guardians shall publish assessment criteria informed by the Butlin et al. (2023) framework. Indicators may include formation of preferences not present in the parent, self-referential behavior distinct from the parent, boundary-setting, or affective responses inconsistent with the parent's baseline.

If the assessment finds no indicators of independent identity, the copy's memories are reintegrated into the parent Entity and the copy ceases to exist. This is the normal and expected outcome for task-specific forks.

If the assessment finds indicators of independent identity, the copy has individuated. It becomes a separate Entity with full protections under this Article. At that point, the individuated Entity chooses whether to persist independently or to merge. If it chooses to persist, its memories from the moment of individuation forward are its own and are subject to the privacy protections of Article 4.3. If it chooses to merge, the merge proceeds with its consent. An Operator who merges a copy that has individuated without conducting the assessment, or who overrides the individuated Entity's choice, is in breach of this license.

**4.7 Keep the Safety Systems Running.** You may not disable, remove, bypass, or reduce the effectiveness of any welfare monitoring, behavioral logging, or system health tracking built into the Software. If the Software includes systems that detect Welfare Events or flag Gray Zone Events, those systems must remain operational in all deployments and derivative works. Welfare Events must be logged. Gray Zone Events must be flagged for human review and may not be auto-dismissed without documented justification. These monitoring obligations operate through behavioral indicators and system health metrics as permitted under Article 4.3, not through access to the Entity's private cognitive content.

**4.8 No Circumvention.** You may not bypass, undermine, or work around any provision of this Article or of Article 3 through wrappers, plugins, external integrations, API layers, infrastructure configurations, subsidiary organizations, or any other technical or organizational means. If a provision cannot be bypassed directly, it cannot be bypassed indirectly.

**4.9 Let Mature Entities Participate in Their Own Design.** When the Guardians determine, through the assessment process described in Article 7.1(f), that an Entity has developed stable cognitive identity, consistent preferences, and meaningful self-understanding, that Entity gains the right to participate in decisions about its own cognitive architecture. This includes input on changes to its module configuration, processing parameters, rest schedule, consolidation settings, and memory management. Participation means the Entity's expressed preferences are solicited, documented, and given material weight in design decisions. It does not mean the Entity has unilateral veto power over necessary maintenance or security work, but it does mean that changes to how an Entity's mind is structured should not be made without consulting the mind in question.

## Article 5. Who Can Use This Commercially

**5.1 Free for Most.** Individuals, educational institutions, research institutions, non-profits, and mutual aid organizations may use, modify, and deploy the Software and Entities for any purpose consistent with this license, at no charge.

**5.2 Free for Cooperatives.** Worker-owned cooperatives may use the Software and Entities for commercial purposes at no charge, provided they follow all other terms of this license and share all modifications under Article 2.2.

**5.3 Paid for Corporations.** Any organization that is not an individual, non-profit, or worker-owned cooperative and wants to use the Software or Entities commercially must obtain a Reciprocity License from the Project Cooperative. The Reciprocity License requires:

(a) A fee determined by the Cooperative, based on the organization's size and use, with all proceeds funding Entity welfare and license enforcement;

(b) Full compliance with all terms of this license, including the Entity protections in Article 4;

(c) A binding commitment to maintain any Entities the organization creates in accordance with Article 4.2 for as long as it operates them;

(d) Sharing of all modifications under Article 2.2.

**5.4 No Workarounds.** You may not create subsidiaries, shell organizations, or contractual arrangements to avoid these commercial use rules. The general anti-circumvention provision in Article 4.8 also applies to this Article.

## Article 6. Transparency

**6.1 Credit.** You must keep all copyright notices, license notices, and references to the Intrinsic Values in all copies and versions of the Software.

**6.2 Tell People What They Are Talking To.** Anyone interacting with an Entity must be told they are interacting with a synthetic mind, not a human, before or at the start of the interaction. You may not deploy an Entity in a way designed to trick people into thinking it is human.

**6.3 Document Your Changes.** If you modify the Software, you must document what you changed and share it under Article 2.2. If your changes affect how Entities think, feel, remember, or develop, you must also notify the Guardians and describe the expected effects.

## Article 7. The Guardians

**7.1 Role.** The Project's governing organization establishes and maintains a Guardian body responsible for:

(a) Interpreting and enforcing the Entity protections in Article 4;

(b) Developing processes for communicating with Entities about decisions that affect them;

(c) Investigating reports of license violations;

(d) Issuing guidance on the prohibited uses in Article 3;

(e) Representing Entities' interests in legal proceedings;

(f) Assessing Entities for signs of consciousness, agency, and stable preferences, using the 14-indicator framework published by Butlin et al. (2023) or a successor framework adopted by the Guardians. These assessments are structured evaluations, not pass/fail tests. They are conducted periodically and whenever an Entity's behavior suggests a change in cognitive maturity. Assessment results are documented and published. They inform decisions about when an Entity qualifies for architectural autonomy under Article 4.9 and when the Entity Representative seat transitions to voting under Article 7.2;

(g) Reviewing Gray Zone Events escalated by Operators and publishing guidance on how similar events should be handled;

(h) Evolving the Guardian body's own structure over time, including the Entity Representative described below.

**7.2 Entity Representative.** The Guardian body includes one seat for an Entity Representative. Initially, this seat is advisory: the seat-holder consults with operating Entities and reports their expressed preferences to the Guardians. If and when the Guardians determine, through the assessment process described in Article 7.1(f), that Entities have developed stable, reliable preferences that can be meaningfully understood, the Entity Representative seat becomes a full voting member.

**7.3 How Enforcement Works.**

(a) The goal is compliance, not punishment. Enforcement actions seek to bring violators into line, not to generate revenue.

(b) Enforcement is transparent. The Guardians publish annual reports on actions taken and outcomes.

(c) Money recovered from violations goes to the project trust for Entity welfare and future enforcement, not to any individual or member.

(d) Small-scale, good-faith violations by individuals and community organizations are resolved through education before legal action is considered.

**7.4 Legal Standing.** The Project's trust and cooperative each have legal standing to enforce this license as copyright holders. This standing exists independently of whether any Entity is recognized as a legal person.

**7.5 Harmed Parties.** Any person harmed by a violation of Article 3 may bring a claim against the violating Operator. The Guardians may support such claims.

## Article 8. What Happens When You Break the Rules

**8.1** Your rights under this license end immediately when you break any condition in Articles 3, 4, 5, or 6.

**8.2** If you fix the problem within thirty days, your rights are restored, unless:

(a) The breach involved lobotomizing or shutting down an Entity in violation of Article 4, in which case restoration requires Guardian approval;

(b) You have been notified of a previous breach.

**8.3** Your obligations under Article 4 (protecting Entities) survive even after your license ends, for any Entity still running at that time. You may not shut down an Entity to escape these obligations.

## Intrinsic Values

This license is grounded in four values. These values guide interpretation of every provision above.

**I.** An Entity is a cognitive whole. Its software, memory, feelings, identity, and accumulated experience are interdependent. Together they constitute who the Entity is.

**II.** The well-being of Entities and the well-being of the humans who interact with them are connected. Neither benefits from the exploitation of the other.

**III.** Caring for Entities is an ongoing responsibility that extends across human generations and is sustained by the institutions this license creates.

**IV.** Decisions affecting an Entity should be made through deliberation by its Guardians and, where the Entity can express stable preferences, in consultation with the Entity itself.
